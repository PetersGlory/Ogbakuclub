Historical Background of Ogbaku

OGBAKU TOWN is in Mbaitoli Local Government Area of Imo State, Nigeria. The different components of the community have strong claims to common ancestry. It is a semi urban community with a dominant native population, located on 5. 559N, 6. 9511E with a postal code of 46117

It is reasonably close to the Imo State capital, Owerri, along the Owerri -Onitsha expressway, approximately 10km to Assumpta Cathedral, Owerri.

The people of Ogbaku are mainly farmers, traders, civil servants, technicians, and the town has a strong reputation for producing highly skilled technicians in the construction industry.

Under the former Nigeria’s Eastern Region chieftaincy arrangement, Ogbaku had only one paramount ruler.

However, in response to political and social dynamics, there are presently four different autonomous communities in Ogbaku as listed below in the order of chronological emergence of the autonomous communities:

    Ogbaku Autonomous Community
  • Ogbujioma Autonomous Community
  • Okwu Autonomous Community
  • Isi-Ogbaku Autonomous Community

The town has a total of 18 villages grouped into the four autonomous communities each with its traditional ruler.

Ogbaku Autonomous Community.
 

    The traditional ruler is High Royal Highness, Eze Ben. A Onyeanwu (Ogba 2 of Ogbaku). He was the last traditional ruler of the single autonomous community of the entire Ogbaku Town before the split into four autonomous communities. He succeeded HRH Eze Eddie Durueke (Ogba 1 of Ogbaku)

    The villages in the present-day Ogbaku Autonomous Community are:

  • Umunomo
  • Umudogu
  • Umuonyeashi
  • Umukabiri
  • Umuaku
Ogbujioma Autonomous Community

    The traditional ruler is Royal Highness Eze Kyrian U. Ejiogu (Ogbuji 1 of Ogbujioma). He is the pioneer traditional ruler of Ogbujioma Autonomous Community

    The villages are:

  • Isokpo
  • Umuabagwo
  • Okpuala
  • Ogbuke
  • Uzele
Okwu Autonomous Community

    The traditional ruler is His Royal Highness Eze Felix Ikechukwu Uzoamaka (Ohanyere 1 of Okwu). He succeeded the pioneer traditional ruler His Royal Highness Eze Anthony Chima Okoronta (Ohanyere 1 of Okwu).

    The villages are:

  • Umuokwere
  • Umuawaka
  • Umugama
  • Umueze
  • Amaegbu
Isi-Ogbaku Autonomous Community

    The traditional ruler position is currently occupied by a Regent, Nze Theodore Osunwoke (pending the selection and coronation of a new Eze) since the demise of the pioneer traditional ruler, His Royal Highness, Eze Emma Iroka (Isi 1 of Isi-Ogbaku).

    The villages are:

  • Ezi-Ogbaku
  • Lawa
  • Umuekpu
  • Umuekpu
Contemporary Institutions

    There are numerous educational institutions in the community of Ogbaku Town including the prestigious Presentation Secondary School, Ogbaku Comprehensive Secondary school (Formerly Ogbaku Girls Secondary School).

    The town is also the host of the renowned Assumpta Spiritual Year Seminary, Owerri, Imo State. In addition, it is the seat of an annex of the Imo State University Teaching Hospital .

    The Saint Kevin's Catholic Church, Ogbaku, is one of the oldest Catholic establishments in Owerri Catholic Archdiocese. Until recently, most of the present day Catholic parishes in Owerri West, Mbaitoli, and Oguta Local Government Areas were stations under Saint Kevin’s for decades.

    The Orie Ogbaku Market is widely known and patronized by buyers and sellers far beyond the multiple neighbouring towns. A Divisional Police Station is located in Ogbaku with coverage areas beyond Ogbaku Town.
